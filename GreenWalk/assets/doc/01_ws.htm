The National Centre for Sustainability and Environmental Design (WS)

Our buildings can have a large impact on our environment from the materials used in construction to the energy and water they use in daily operation. New buildings have to meet a number of energy and water efficiency standards. This sustainability building features:

  • Passive solar design that shades the building from hot summer sun and lets winter sun in to warm the building.
  • A high percentage of recycled and low energy intensive materials used in construction.
  • Natural sunlight usage is complimented by an energy efficient lighting system.
  • Energy efficient heating and cooling systems utilising thermal chimneys and natural ventilation.
  • Large earth banks to increase the buildings thermal mass helping to keep the building at a constant temperature.
  • Grid connected photovoltaic panels generating electricity without producing greenhouse gas emissions. (Rated at 3kW)
  • A 90,000 litre rainwater tank reduces storm water runoff and supplies water for toilets and gardens.
  • Bicycle racks, showers and change rooms to encourage sustainable transport and exercise.
  • Our Smart Automation Training Laboratory where students learn to use the latest technology to model efficient manufacturing processes.
