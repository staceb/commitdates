Rain garden, drainage swale & Renewable Energy Solar Training Area

This demonstration rain garden and drainage swale captures rainwater runoff. Their functions are to slow the rate of rainwater going into the storm water system, which reduces flooding and erosion in our rivers and streams. The combination of the sand filter and microbes associated with plant roots clean the water by ‘eating up’ pollutants which helps keep our rivers and streams healthy. This system mimics the natural designs and functions of creeks and wetlands.

The Renewable Energy Solar Training Area located up the hill next to the wind turbine allows students to design stand alone power supply systems and safely simulate solar panel installations. You too can learn about photovoltaic (solar) panel installation or grid connected systems by enquiring at Swinburne about one of our renewable energy courses.
