The National Centre for Sustainability and Environmental Design (WS)

Multi level commercial building

  • Daylight harvesting
  • Smart programmable hardware
  • Occupancy sensing
  • LED lighting featuring dimmable technology
  • Connectivity to Building Automation Systems (BAS) and Heating, Ventilating, and Air Conditioning (HVAC) systems
  • Integration with Supervisory Control and Data Acquisition (SCADA) systems
  • Advanced capabilities enable remote graphical modelling from the Industrial Automation Facility in the Sustainability Building (WS303)

Lighting control system using Philips Dynalite technology

Enables modelling across 4 levels:

  1. Carpark
  2. Lobby
  3. Office space
  4. Boardroom

Students are able to develop projects that control an individual area or simulate changes across the whole system. Additionally, students can simulate parameters for smart energy efficient homes.

Competency based skill sets in lighting management systems will be available as a stand alone short course or integrated into existing qualifications
